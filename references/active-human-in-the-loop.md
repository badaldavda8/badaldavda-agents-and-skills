# Reference: Being an Active Human in the Loop

A quick checklist when working with AI on anything that matters.

---

## Before you start

- [ ] Write down the core assumption you are bringing into this conversation
- [ ] Write down what you want the output to look like — before AI produces it
- [ ] Note what you would do if AI gets it wrong

---

## While AI works

- [ ] Keep a notebook or text file open alongside the conversation
- [ ] Write down what AI is doing at each step — in your own words, not a copy
- [ ] Flag anything that feels too smooth, too agreeable, too fast
- [ ] When you stop being able to summarise what AI just said — stop. Ask a question.

---

## After AI responds

- [ ] Can you explain the reasoning in your own words?
- [ ] What assumptions did AI make that you did not explicitly give it?
- [ ] Does the conclusion follow from the evidence, or is it floating?
- [ ] Ask: *"What assumptions did you make? Show me the key ones."*
- [ ] Ask: *"Summarise what you just did as a mind map."*

---

## Warning signs you have drifted

- You are reading AI output the way you used to copy notes from a board — eyes moving, brain off
- You have been in a conversation for 30+ minutes and cannot describe what AI has decided on your behalf
- AI has agreed with everything you said, including the things you were not sure about
- The output is very long, very structured, and you have not pushed back once

---

## The two-tool fix

**Tool 1 — The notebook:** Write down your assumptions, track AI's steps, reconstruct its reasoning in your own words. The reconstruction is not for the notes. The reconstruction is the understanding.

**Tool 2 — The mind map:** Ask AI: *"Show me your reasoning as a flowchart"* or *"What assumptions did you make and what conclusions did they lead to?"* A diagram of AI's thinking makes the invisible visible. You will spot in thirty seconds what you would miss in three readings of the prose.
