## The research behind the process

### Active recall
The science behind why reconstruction makes understanding stick. Not re-reading, not highlighting — the act of rebuilding from your own understanding is what creates the understanding. Popularised by research on retrieval practice; referenced in Roediger & Butler (2011).

### AI sycophancy
Large language models are structurally designed to produce contextually agreeable responses. They are optimised to make you feel heard. A 2025 paper confirmed this is a design property, not a bug: models produce "contextually agreeable responses over balanced ones."

### Context anchoring
Research found that wrong information introduced early in a multi-turn conversation caused an average 39% performance drop (arxiv.org/pdf/2505.06120). The model anchors to what was said early and cannot fully recover even when corrected later.

### The jagged frontier (Mollick)
Users cannot locate where AI competence ends and incompetence begins, so they defer everywhere — including where AI is confidently wrong. Ethan Mollick's framing from research with BCG consultants.

### The UPenn finding
A University of Pennsylvania study (SSRN working paper, 2024 — not yet peer-reviewed at time of writing) found that students using ChatGPT solved 48% more practice problems but scored 17% lower on tests of actual conceptual understanding. Doing more. Understanding less.

## The storytellers this skill is validated against

| Storyteller | Key principle borrowed |
|---|---|
| Anne Lamott | Find the avoided sentence first — it is almost always the real subject |
| Joan Didion | Write to find out what you are afraid of |
| Malcolm Gladwell | Instinct before research — know your own mind first |
| Kurt Vonnegut | Every structural decision is for the reader, not the writer |
| Mary Karr | Readers inhabit a body in a place, not an idea in the abstract |
| Robert McKee | Without stakes, there is no story — only a reflection |
| Morgan Housel | The quiet integrity beat — what you do when nobody is watching |
| John McPhee | The opening image must pay off in the closing |
| Gay Talese | Direct speech puts readers in the room. Paraphrase keeps them outside. |
| Brené Brown | The sentence you are most reluctant to write is the center |
